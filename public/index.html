Phase 1A

Phase 1B
In an early release of Phase 1B, frontline health care workers, nursing home and assisted living residents, police and fire personnel and people ages 65 and older became eligible vaccine. Part two of the release includes:
  • Education and child care workers
  • People in Medicaid long-term care programs such as IRIS and Family Care
  • Public-facing essential workers, such as 911 operators, public transit workers, utility workers and food supply chain workers, including agricultural workers and retail food workers
  • Non-frontline health care personnel
  • People in congregate living, such as those in mental health institutions or people who are incarcerated
  • Mink husbandry workers
Phase 1C
Will likely to include people ages 16 to 64 with high-risk medical conditions, non-frontline health care workers, flight attendants, librarians and veterinarians have all been discussed.
Phase 2
This phase is under discussion, but is likely to include everyone else 16 years and older.
